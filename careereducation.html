Local Chapter Involvement

In my past three years as a member of Cupertino FBLA, I have significantly contributed to the chapter. All of my contributions as well as officer positions demonstrate my leadership, organization, and team-work skills, which are all crucial for the program management profession.

  1. Competitions Officer (2016 - 2017)
    • This position has allowed for me to learn how to lead about 150-200 Cupertino FBLA members to section, state, and national level competitions. Throughout this leadership occupation, I have been able to lead weekly study sessions, mock-competitions, and speak in front of a large audience of competitors. All the competitors rely on me for further guidance in their competitions and providence of resources. With this experience, I have developed thorough leadership skills as well as organizational skills.
  2. Partnership with Business Committee Member (2016 - 2017)
    • As a committee member for our annual Partnership with Business Project, I have gained both skills in being detail oriented and working with team-mates. In this committee, our chapter collaborated with Chegg, an educational prep center, to promote their services as well as provide another source of education to Cupertino High School students. Personally, I worked with my fellow committee members to create the project report, make and promote surveys, and craft promotional flyers to post around campus. Through this occupation, I was able to learn how to collaborate with others and be detail oriented to easily convey information.
  3. American Enterprise Committee (2015 - 2016)
    • As a committee member for the American Enterprise Committee, I have obtained several skills including team-work and leadership. As a committee, we have visited several places to volunteer and educate students about the American Enterprise system. We visit our local elementary schools bi-weekly to present about the American Enterprise System and work with each other to create and enhance these presentations. In addition, I collaborated with fellow committee members to create the annual project report, which is a portfolio of our achievements throughout the school year. This role has helped me become someone with remarkable leadership and team-work skills because of the various activities we did.
